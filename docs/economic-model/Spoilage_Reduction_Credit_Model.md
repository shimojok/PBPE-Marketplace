# Spoilage Reduction Credit Model (SRC)

The Spoilage Reduction Credit (SRC) quantifies reductions in postharvest  
losses achieved through MBT55-driven microbial stabilization, oxidation  
suppression, moisture control, and cold chain optimization.  
SRC is a core PBPE credit connecting food systems to climate finance.

---

# 1. Overview

Food spoilage is driven by:

- Mold and fungal growth
- Bacterial contamination
- Oxidation
- Moisture imbalance
- Temperature fluctuations
- Poor storage conditions

MBT55 reduces spoilage across fresh produce, grains, meat, dairy, and aquaculture.

---

# 2. MBT55 Spoilage Reduction Mechanisms

## 2.1 Organic Acid Suppression
MBT55 produces:

- Lactic acid
- Acetic acid
- Propionic acid

These reduce spoilage microbes.

---

## 2.2 Redox Stabilization
MBT55 produces:

- Quinones
- Phenolics
- Antioxidant metabolites

These suppress oxidation and browning.

---

## 2.3 Moisture and Hydration Control
MBT55 stabilizes:

- Water activity
- Surface hydration
- Osmotic balance

This reduces soft rot and texture loss.

---

## 2.4 Cold Chain Stability
MBT55 improves:

- Microbial stability at low temperatures
- Condensation control
- Aroma retention

---

# 3. SRC Calculation

\[
SRC = (Loss_{baseline} - Loss_{after}) \times V \times SF
\]

Where:

- \(Loss_{baseline}\) = spoilage before MBT55  
- \(Loss_{after}\) = spoilage after MBT55  
- \(V\) = volume of food handled  
- \(SF\) = spoilage financial factor
