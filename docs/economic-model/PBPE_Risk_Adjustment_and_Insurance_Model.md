# PBPE Risk Adjustment and Insurance Model

The PBPE Risk Adjustment and Insurance Model protects farmers, enterprises,  
and investors from biological, climate, and market volatility.  
It ensures stability and confidence in PBPE credit generation.

---

# 1. Overview

PBPE risk management covers:

- Biological risk  
- Climate variability  
- Market price volatility  
- Verification uncertainty  
- Regional instability  

---

# 2. Risk Categories

## 2.1 Biological Risk
- Crop disease outbreaks  
- Livestock health shocks  
- Water contamination events  

## 2.2 Climate Risk
- Drought  
- Flooding  
- Extreme temperature  

## 2.3 Market Risk
- Price crashes  
- Liquidity shortages  
- Buyer withdrawal  

## 2.4 Verification Risk
- Data gaps  
- Methodology uncertainty  
- Evidence inconsistency  

---

# 3. Risk Adjustment Factor (RAF)

Each PBPE credit receives a RAF:

RAF = Base_Risk × Regional_Risk × Data_Quality_Risk × Climate_Risk

Applied as:

Adjusted_Credit_Value = Raw_Value × (1 − RAF)

Typical RAF range: **0.05–0.25**

---

# 4. PBPE Insurance Mechanisms

PBPE offers:

## 4.1 Minimum Revenue Guarantee (MRG)
- Ensures minimum payout per credit  
- Backed by insurance pool  

## 4.2 Biological Shock Insurance
- Covers disease outbreaks  
- Covers livestock mortality events  

## 4.3 Climate Variability Insurance
- Covers drought/flood losses  
- Index-based triggers  

## 4.4 Market Stability Insurance
- Protects against price crashes  
- Activates circuit-breaker payouts  

---

# 5. Insurance Pool Funding

Sources:

- Marketplace fees  
- Government climate funds  
- ESG investors  
- Reinsurance partners  

---

# 6. Example Scenario

A farmer expected 200 credits but produced 150 due to drought.

Insurance payout:

Shortfall = 200 − 150 = 50 credits Payout = 50 × Guaranteed_Floor_Price
