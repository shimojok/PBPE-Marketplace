# Carbon Credit and Soil Carbon Model (ΔC‑SCC)

The Soil Carbon Credit (ΔC‑SCC) quantifies carbon sequestration driven by MBT55  
through humification, organic acid pathways, redox polymerization, and  
microaggregate stabilization.  
This is the core climate credit in the PBPE system.

---

# 1. Overview

Soil carbon increases through:

- Organic matter input  
- Microbial transformation  
- Humification  
- Mineral association  
- Aggregate stabilization  

MBT55 accelerates all stages.

---

# 2. Carbon Sequestration Mechanisms

## 2.1 Organic Acid Pathways
MBT55 produces:

- Lactic acid  
- Acetic acid  
- Propionic acid  
- Butyric acid  

These initiate carbon transformation.

---

## 2.2 Humification
MBT55 converts organic matter into:

- Fulvic acids  
- Humic acids  
- Recalcitrant carbon complexes  

---

## 2.3 Redox Polymerization
Redox-active microbes drive:

- Mn/Fe cycling  
- Quinone polymerization  
- Electron shuttling  

This stabilizes carbon for decades.

---

## 2.4 Microaggregate Formation
MBT55 increases:

- Soil aggregation  
- Mineral-associated organic carbon  
- Water-stable aggregates

# 3. ΔC‑SCC Calculation

\[
ΔC\_{SCC} = (C_{after} - C_{before}) \times A \times CF
\]

Where:

- \(C_{after}\) = soil carbon after MBT55  
- \(C_{before}\) = baseline soil carbon  
- \(A\) = land area  
- \(CF\) = carbon financial factor
