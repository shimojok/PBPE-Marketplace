# PBPE Financial Flows

The PBPE Financial Flows model describes how money, credits,  
and incentives move through the PBPE Marketplace.  
It defines the economic plumbing that connects farmers, enterprises,  
verifiers, insurers, and national climate programs.

---

# 1. Overview

PBPE financial flows include:

- Credit issuance  
- Credit sales  
- Revenue distribution  
- Insurance payouts  
- Verification fees  
- Marketplace fees  
- Government incentive flows  

These flows ensure transparency and economic stability.

---

# 2. Core Financial Actors

- Farmers / Producers  
- Enterprises (ESG buyers)  
- Verifiers  
- Marketplace operator  
- Insurance pool  
- Government climate programs  
- Data providers (labs, sensors)  

---

# 3. Financial Flow Architecture

[Farmer] → Issues Credits ↓ [Marketplace] → Lists Credits ↓ [Enterprise Buyer] → Purchases Credits ↓ [Revenue Distribution Engine] ↓ [Farmer / Verifier / Marketplace / Insurance Pool]

---

# 4. Credit Sale Flow

1. Credits issued  
2. Credits listed on marketplace  
3. Buyer places order  
4. Smart contract executes settlement  
5. Revenue distributed automatically  
6. Registry updated  

---

# 5. Revenue Distribution Flow

Total_Revenue = Credit_Price × Volume

Distributed as:

Farmer: 60–75% Verifier: 3–7% Marketplace: 5–10% Insurance Pool: 5–10% Government Program: 5–15%

---

# 6. Insurance Flow

Triggered when:

- Biological shocks  
- Climate events  
- Market crashes  
- Verification failures  

Insurance payout:

Payout = Shortfall × Guaranteed_Floor_Price

---

# 7. Government Incentive Flow

Governments may:

- Subsidize verification  
- Provide price floors  
- Purchase credits directly  
- Fund insurance pools  

---

# 8. Example Scenario

A buyer purchases 300 PBPE credits at $50 each:

Total Revenue = $15,000 Farmer (70%): $10,500 Verifier (5%): $750 Marketplace (10%): $1,500 Insurance Pool (10%): $1,500
