# Quality Premium Credit Model (QPC)

The Quality Premium Credit (QPC) quantifies improvements in food quality  
driven by MBT55, including nutrient retention, flavor stability, texture  
preservation, and reduced oxidation.  
QPC connects biological quality improvements to financial value.

---

# 1. Overview

Food quality declines due to:

- Oxidation
- Moisture imbalance
- Nutrient degradation
- Microbial spoilage
- Temperature stress

MBT55 stabilizes quality across produce, grains, meat, dairy, and processed foods.

---

# 2. MBT55 Quality Improvement Mechanisms

## 2.1 Oxidation Suppression
MBT55 produces:

- Quinones
- Phenolics
- Antioxidant metabolites

These reduce:

- Browning
- Aroma loss
- Vitamin degradation

---

## 2.2 Nutrient Preservation
MBT55 stabilizes:

- Vitamins (C, B-complex)
- Polyphenols
- Amino acids
- Carotenoids
- Essential fatty acids

---

## 2.3 Texture and Moisture Stability
MBT55 improves:

- Water activity balance
- Cell wall integrity
- Firmness retention

---

## 2.4 Flavor and Aroma Protection
MBT55 reduces:

- Off-flavors
- Fermentation spoilage
- Oxidative aroma loss

---

# 3. QPC Calculation

\[
QPC = (Q_{after} - Q_{before}) \times V \times QF
\]

Where:

- \(Q_{after}\) = quality score after MBT55  
- \(Q_{before}\) = baseline quality score  
- \(V\) = volume of food  
- \(QF\) = quality financial factor
