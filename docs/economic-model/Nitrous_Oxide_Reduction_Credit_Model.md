# Nitrous Oxide Reduction Credit Model (NRC)

The Nitrous Oxide Reduction Credit (NRC) quantifies reductions in N₂O emissions  
achieved through MBT55-driven nitrogen stabilization, microbial nitrogen retention,  
and suppression of nitrification–denitrification spikes.  
NRC is one of the highest-value climate credits in PBPE.

---

# 1. Overview

N₂O emissions originate from:

- Nitrification
- Denitrification
- Excess fertilizer
- Anaerobic soil pockets
- Microbial imbalance

N₂O has **298×** the warming potential of CO₂,  
making its reduction extremely valuable.

---

# 2. MBT55 N₂O Reduction Mechanisms

## 2.1 Organic Acid Regulation
Organic acids:

- Slow nitrification
- Reduce nitrate accumulation
- Stabilize soil pH microzones

---

## 2.2 Microbial Nitrogen Retention
MBT55 increases:

- Microbial biomass nitrogen (MBN)
- Amino-N retention
- Organic-N formation

This keeps nitrogen **in the soil**, not in the atmosphere.

---

## 2.3 Redox Cycling
MBT55 stabilizes nitrate by:

- Mn/Fe cycling
- Electron shuttling
- Reducing denitrification spikes

---

## 2.4 Soil Structure Improvement
Better aggregation reduces:

- Anaerobic pockets
- N₂O hotspots

---

# 3. NRC Calculation

\[
NRC = (N2O_{baseline} - N2O_{after}) \times A \times NF
\]

Where:

- \(N2O_{baseline}\) = emissions before MBT55  
- \(N2O_{after}\) = emissions after MBT55  
- \(A\) = land area  
- \(NF\) = nitrogen financial factor
