# PBPE Incentive and Revenue Sharing Model

The PBPE Incentive and Revenue Sharing Model defines how value generated  
by PBPE credits is distributed among farmers, enterprises, verifiers,  
market operators, and national programs.  
It ensures fair compensation and long-term ecosystem participation.

---

# 1. Overview

PBPE incentives are designed to:

- Reward biological improvements  
- Support smallholder farmers  
- Encourage long-term adoption  
- Maintain verifier independence  
- Ensure marketplace sustainability  

---

# 2. Revenue Sources

PBPE revenue comes from:

- Credit sales  
- Enterprise procurement  
- Government climate programs  
- Insurance payouts  
- Premium quality markets  
- Marketplace transaction fees  

---

# 3. Revenue Distribution Model

Farmer / Producer: 60–75% Verifier: 3–7% Marketplace Operator: 5–10% Data Providers (Labs/Sensors): 2–5% Local Government / Program: 5–15%


Percentages vary by region and credit type.

---

# 4. Incentive Types

## 4.1 Adoption Incentives
- Upfront micro-grants  
- Reduced verification fees  
- Free baseline assessments  

## 4.2 Performance Incentives
- Higher payouts for high-quality data  
- Bonuses for multi-year participation  
- Premiums for A-rated credits  

## 4.3 Risk Mitigation Incentives
- Insurance-backed minimum payouts  
- Stabilized price floors  
- Guaranteed buyer-of-last-resort programs  

---

# 5. Multi-Year Incentive Curve

PBPE rewards long-term participation:

Year 1: Base payout Year 2: +10% loyalty bonus Year 3: +15% performance bonus Year 4+: +20% stability bonus

---

# 6. Example Scenario

A 50-hectare farm:

- Issues 180 PBPE credits  
- Market price: $48/credit  
- Revenue: $8,640  
- Farmer share (70%): $6,048  
- Verifier share (5%): $432  
- Marketplace share (10%): $864  

---

# 7. One-Sentence Summary

**PBPE incentives ensure fair, transparent, and long-term value distribution  
that rewards biological improvement and marketplace participation.**
