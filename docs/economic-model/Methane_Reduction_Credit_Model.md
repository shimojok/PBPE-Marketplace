# Methane Reduction Credit Model (MRC)

The Methane Reduction Credit (MRC) quantifies reductions in CH₄ emissions  
achieved through MBT55-driven microbial optimization, fermentation shifts,  
and methanotroph activation.  
MRC is a core climate credit within the PBPE system.

---

# 1. Overview

Methane (CH₄) is produced in:

- Ruminant digestion
- Anaerobic soils
- Rice paddies
- Manure and slurry
- Organic waste piles

CH₄ has **84×** the warming potential of CO₂ (20-year GWP),  
making its reduction a high-value climate asset.

---

# 2. MBT55 Methane Reduction Mechanisms

## 2.1 Fermentation Shift Toward SCFA
MBT55 increases:

- Acetate
- Propionate
- Butyrate

This reduces hydrogen availability for methanogenesis.

---

## 2.2 Methanotroph Activation
MBT55 stimulates:

- Type I methanotrophs
- Type II methanotrophs

These microbes **consume methane** as an energy source.

---

## 2.3 Redox Cycling
Mn/Fe cycling:

- Competes with methanogenesis
- Provides alternative electron sinks
- Reduces anaerobic hotspots

---

## 2.4 Aerobic Microzone Formation
MBT55 increases:

- Aerobic bacteria
- Oxygen diffusion
- Surface oxidation

This suppresses methane-producing archaea.

---

# 3. MRC Calculation

\[
MRC = (CH4_{baseline} - CH4_{after}) \times A \times MF
\]

Where:

- \(CH4_{baseline}\) = methane emissions before MBT55  
- \(CH4_{after}\) = methane emissions after MBT55  
- \(A\) = system scale (animals, hectares, ponds)  
- \(MF\) = methane financial factor
