# PBPE Marketplace Whitepaper (Draft)

## 1. Introduction

The PBPE Marketplace is an open, verifiable, and interoperable infrastructure
for Positive Bio-Physical Externalities (PBPE). It is designed to connect
regenerative ecological activities with financial markets through measurable,
auditable, and programmable impact units.

This document is a **draft whitepaper** and will evolve as the architecture,
governance, and implementation details are refined.

## 2. Problem Statement

Conventional markets systematically undervalue or ignore positive ecological
externalities. Regenerative activities such as soil restoration, watershed
protection, biodiversity enhancement, and community health improvements are
rarely priced, settled, or integrated into financial products.

Key issues include:

- Lack of standardized, verifiable impact units  
- Fragmented data sources and methodologies  
- Limited interoperability between ecological data and financial systems  
- Absence of transparent, auditable settlement mechanisms  

The PBPE Marketplace addresses these gaps.

## 3. PBPE Concept

Positive Bio-Physical Externalities (PBPE) are **measurable, location-specific,
and time-bounded ecological improvements** that can be:

- Monitored (via sensors, models, or verified reports)  
- Quantified using transparent methodologies  
- Tokenized into digital units  
- Settled and composed into financial products  

PBPEs are not generic “offsets” but **context-aware impact primitives**.

## 4. System Architecture (High-Level)

The PBPE Marketplace architecture consists of:

- **Impact Data Layer**: ingestion of ecological and contextual data  
- **PBPE Impact Engine**: transformation of raw data into PBPE units  
- **Ledger Layer**: recording PBPE issuance, ownership, and settlement  
- **Rating & Risk Layer**: evaluation of PBPE quality, durability, and risk  
- **Marketplace & UI**: discovery, composition, and interaction interfaces  
- **Integration Layer**: APIs and SDKs for external systems and partners  

See: `docs/architecture/pbpe-architecture.png` for the visual architecture.

## 5. Core Design Principles

- **Verifiability**: every PBPE unit must be traceable and auditable  
- **Interoperability**: open standards and APIs for external integration  
- **Composability**: PBPE units can be bundled into higher-order products  
- **Governance-ready**: suitable for regulators, public institutions, and
  institutional investors  
- **Ecology-first**: financial structures must not undermine ecological integrity
