# Agriculture and Soil Regeneration Use Case

This use case demonstrates how MBT55 and PBPE credits transform  
agricultural soil regeneration into measurable financial value.  
Farmers gain new revenue streams while improving soil health,  
carbon sequestration, and climate resilience.

---

# 1. Overview

MBT55 improves:

- Soil carbon sequestration  
- Soil structure and aggregation  
- Water retention  
- Nutrient cycling  
- Microbial diversity  
- Crop resilience  

These improvements generate PBPE credits.

---

# 2. PBPE Credits Generated

Agriculture produces:

- **ΔC‑SCC**（Soil Carbon Credit）  
- **NRC**（Nitrous Oxide Reduction Credit）  
- **DRC**（Disease Reduction Credit）  
- **WQC**（Water Quality Credit）  
- **QPC**（Quality Premium Credit）  

---

# 3. Data Inputs

Required data:

- Soil carbon baseline  
- Fertilizer usage  
- Soil moisture  
- Microbial activity  
- Crop yield  
- Satellite NDVI  
- Lab carbon analysis  

---

# 4. Workflow

1. **MBT55 applied to soil**  
2. **Soil carbon increases**  
3. **GHG emissions decrease**  
4. **Impact data collected**  
5. **PBPE credits issued**  
6. **Credits sold on marketplace**  
7. **Farmer receives revenue**  

---

# 5. Financial Impact

Farmers gain:

- Carbon revenue  
- Fertilizer savings  
- Yield stability  
- Soil resilience  
- Reduced disease losses  

---

# 6. Example Scenario

A 50-hectare farm in Chiba:

- Soil carbon increases by 0.8 tC/ha  
- N₂O emissions drop by 12%  
- Disease incidence drops by 18%  

Credits issued:

- 146 tCO₂e ΔC‑SCC  
- 22 tCO₂e NRC  
- 18 DRC units  

---

# 7. One-Sentence Summary

**PBPE converts soil regeneration into financial value, enabling farmers  
to earn revenue while restoring agricultural ecosystems.**
