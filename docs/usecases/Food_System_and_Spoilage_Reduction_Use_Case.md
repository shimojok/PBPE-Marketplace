# Food System and Spoilage Reduction Use Case

This use case demonstrates how MBT55 reduces food spoilage across  
fresh produce, grains, meat, dairy, and processed foods, converting  
these improvements into PBPE credits.  
Food companies gain financial value while reducing waste and improving quality.

---

# 1. Overview

MBT55 improves:

- Microbial stability  
- Oxidation resistance  
- Moisture balance  
- Shelf-life extension  
- Cold chain stability  
- Nutrient preservation  

These improvements generate PBPE credits.

---

# 2. PBPE Credits Generated

Food systems produce:

- **SRC**（Spoilage Reduction Credit）  
- **QPC**（Quality Premium Credit）  
- **DRC**（Disease Reduction Credit for postharvest pathogens）  

---

# 3. Data Inputs

Required data:

- Baseline spoilage rates  
- Temperature logs  
- Microbial load data  
- Moisture activity  
- Shelf-life tests  
- Retail shrinkage data  
- Cold chain monitoring  

---

# 4. Workflow

1. **MBT55 applied to produce, grains, or processing water**  
2. **Spoilage microbes suppressed**  
3. **Shelf-life extended**  
4. **Quality metrics improve**  
5. **Impact data collected**  
6. **PBPE credits issued**  
7. **Credits sold to ESG buyers**  

---

# 5. Financial Impact

Food companies gain:

- Reduced shrinkage losses  
- Higher product quality  
- Lower cold chain costs  
- Premium pricing opportunities  
- PBPE credit revenue  

---

# 6. Example Scenario

A fresh produce distributor:

- Spoilage reduced by 22%  
- Shelf-life extended by 3.4 days  
- Microbial load reduced by 41%  

Credits issued:

- 128 SRC units  
- 64 QPC units  

---

# 7. One-Sentence Summary

**PBPE converts spoilage reduction and quality improvements into financial  
credits, enabling climate-positive and waste-free food systems.**
