# Supply Chain and ESG Reporting Use Case

This use case demonstrates how PBPE credits integrate into enterprise  
supply chains and ESG reporting frameworks, enabling companies to  
quantify biological improvements, reduce emissions, and meet regulatory  
and investor expectations.

---

# 1. Overview

Enterprises use PBPE to:

- Measure Scope 1, 2, and 3 reductions  
- Improve supply chain transparency  
- Quantify regenerative agriculture impact  
- Reduce food loss and waste  
- Strengthen ESG reporting  
- Meet CSRD, TCFD, and SEC climate disclosure rules  

PBPE provides standardized, verifiable impact data.

---

# 2. PBPE Credits Generated

Supply chains generate:

- **ΔC‑SCC**（Soil Carbon Credit）  
- **MRC**（Methane Reduction Credit）  
- **NRC**（Nitrous Oxide Reduction Credit）  
- **SRC**（Spoilage Reduction Credit）  
- **QPC**（Quality Premium Credit）  
- **WQC**（Water Quality Credit）  

---

# 3. Data Inputs

Required data:

- Supplier-level emissions  
- Soil carbon and fertilizer data  
- Livestock methane data  
- Cold chain temperature logs  
- Food loss and shrinkage data  
- Water quality metrics  
- Satellite and sensor data  

---

# 4. Workflow

1. **Suppliers adopt MBT55**  
2. **Biological improvements occur**  
3. **PBPE credits issued to suppliers**  
4. **Credits transferred to enterprise buyers**  
5. **Enterprise aggregates credits**  
6. **ESG reports automatically generated**  

---

# 5. Financial Impact

Enterprises gain:

- Verified Scope 3 reductions  
- Lower sustainability reporting costs  
- Improved ESG ratings  
- Supply chain resilience  
- Reduced waste and emissions  

---

# 6. Example Scenario

A multinational food company:

- Reduces spoilage by 14%  
- Cuts Scope 3 methane by 9%  
- Improves soil carbon across 12,000 hectares  

Credits aggregated:

- 18,400 tCO₂e ΔC‑SCC  
- 6,200 tCO₂e MRC  
- 2,900 SRC units  

---

# 7. One-Sentence Summary

**PBPE enables enterprises to quantify biological improvements, reduce  
Scope 3 emissions, and automate ESG reporting across global supply chains.**
