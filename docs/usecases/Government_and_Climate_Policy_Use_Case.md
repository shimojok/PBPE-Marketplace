# Government and Climate Policy Use Case

This use case demonstrates how governments use PBPE credits to support  
national climate goals, regenerative agriculture programs, food security  
initiatives, and biosecurity monitoring.  
PBPE provides a unified framework for policy implementation and impact tracking.

---

# 1. Overview

Governments use PBPE to:

- Track national GHG reductions  
- Incentivize soil carbon sequestration  
- Reduce methane and N₂O emissions  
- Improve food system resilience  
- Monitor biosecurity threats  
- Support regenerative agriculture  
- Reduce water pollution  

PBPE provides standardized, verifiable impact data.

---

# 2. PBPE Credits Used in Policy

Governments rely on:

- **ΔC‑SCC**（Soil Carbon Credit）  
- **MRC**（Methane Reduction Credit）  
- **NRC**（Nitrous Oxide Reduction Credit）  
- **DRC**（Disease Reduction Credit）  
- **WQC**（Water Quality Credit）  

---

# 3. Data Inputs

Required data:

- National soil carbon maps  
- Livestock methane inventories  
- Fertilizer usage data  
- Water quality monitoring  
- Food loss statistics  
- Satellite and sensor networks  
- Biosecurity surveillance  

---

# 4. Policy Workflow

1. **Government launches incentive program**  
2. **Farmers adopt MBT55**  
3. **Biological improvements measured**  
4. **PBPE credits issued**  
5. **Credits aggregated at national level**  
6. **Government reports progress to UNFCCC**  

---

# 5. Policy Impact

Governments gain:

- Lower climate mitigation costs  
- Accurate national GHG accounting  
- Improved food security  
- Reduced disease outbreaks  
- Cleaner water systems  
- Stronger rural economies  

---

# 6. Example Scenario

A national climate program:

- Soil carbon increases by 0.6 tC/ha  
- Methane reduced by 11%  
- N₂O reduced by 7%  
- Water quality improved in 3 major river basins  

Credits aggregated:

- 2.4 million tCO₂e ΔC‑SCC  
- 1.1 million tCO₂e MRC  
- 420,000 tCO₂e NRC  

---

# 7. One-Sentence Summary

**PBPE enables governments to achieve climate goals, support regenerative  
agriculture, and monitor national biosecurity through verifiable impact credits.**
