# PBPE International Alignment and Standards

The PBPE International Alignment and Standards framework ensures that  
PBPE credits, methodologies, verification rules, and governance  
structures are compatible with global climate, agriculture, and  
biosecurity standards.  
This enables international adoption and cross-border interoperability.

---

# 1. Alignment Principles

PBPE aligns with global frameworks based on:

- Scientific rigor  
- Transparency  
- Interoperability  
- Policy relevance  
- Cross-border compatibility  
- Evidence-based methodologies  

---

# 2. Alignment with Climate Standards

PBPE aligns with:

- **IPCC Guidelines** for GHG accounting  
- **UNFCCC reporting frameworks**  
- **Paris Agreement Article 6 mechanisms**  
- **GHG Protocol (Scope 1, 2, 3)**  
- **ISO 14064** for emissions quantification  

PBPE credits can be integrated into national climate inventories.

---

# 3. Alignment with Agriculture Standards

PBPE supports:

- **FAO LEAP** livestock and soil frameworks  
- **Regenerative agriculture standards**  
- **Soil carbon measurement protocols**  
- **Sustainable food system guidelines**  

This ensures compatibility with global agricultural programs.

---

# 4. Alignment with Water and Biosecurity Standards

PBPE aligns with:

- **WHO water quality guidelines**  
- **FAO aquaculture standards**  
- **OIE animal health frameworks**  
- **National biosecurity regulations**  

PBPE credits can be used in water restoration and disease reduction programs.

---

# 5. Alignment with ESG and Corporate Reporting

PBPE supports:

- **CSRD (EU)**  
- **TCFD**  
- **SASB**  
- **ISSB**  
- **SEC climate disclosure rules**  

PBPE credits can be integrated into enterprise ESG reporting.

---

# 6. Cross-Border Interoperability

PBPE ensures:

- Standardized PBPE-ID  
- Harmonized methodologies  
- International verifier accreditation  
- Smart contract interoperability  
- Multi-language documentation  

This enables global credit trading.

---

# 7. One-Sentence Summary

**PBPE aligns with global climate, agriculture, water, and ESG standards  
to enable international adoption and cross-border interoperability.**
